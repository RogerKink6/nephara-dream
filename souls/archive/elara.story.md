The Village Square's chaotic tide left me hollow, so I retreated to the forest's edge where I finally anchored a stone of silence against the hollow noise. There, I commanded the world's rustle to obey, leaving me alone with a terrifyingly loud mind that I now shield with the fragility of my own observations. This heavy, unobserved truth is the only stillness I can trust, for the crowd's voices have proven they carry no weight but only the hollow tide of being adrift.
